COMPLAINT FOR DAMAGES DUE TO WORKPLACE HARASSMENT

The Plaintiff, Maria Santos (hereinafter referred to as "Plaintiff"), by and through her counsel, Atty. Ricardo M. DeLeon, most respectfully states:

I. PARTIES TO THE COMPLAINT

1. Plaintiff, Maria Santos, is a resident of Quezon City and is currently employed as a marketing executive at XYZ Corporation.

2. Defendant, John Smith, is an employee at XYZ Corporation, holding the position of senior analyst, with a business address at XYZ Corporation, Quezon City.


II. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Honorable Court has jurisdiction over this matter under the relevant provisions of the Anti-Sexual Harassment Act (RA 7877) and the Labor Code of the Philippines, as the acts complained of occurred within its territorial jurisdiction.

4. Venue is proper in this Court as the events leading to this complaint transpired within Quezon City.


III. STATEMENT OF FACTS

5. Since January 2024, the Plaintiff has been subjected to persistent harassment by the Defendant during their employment at XYZ Corporation.

6. The Defendant has made numerous derogatory comments about the Plaintiff's work performance, publicly belittling her contributions during team meetings and undermining her professional capabilities.

7. Additionally, the Defendant has sent unsolicited text messages to the Plaintiff outside of working hours, making intrusive inquiries about her personal life and expressing inappropriate personal interest.

8. On several occasions, the Defendant has engaged in unwanted physical contact, including attempts to hug the Plaintiff and unwelcome comments of a sexual nature.

9. After reporting these incidents to the human resources department, the Plaintiff faced retaliation from the Defendant, which included being excluded from important meetings and being the subject of unfounded rumors concerning her professional integrity.

10. As a direct result of the Defendant's actions, the Plaintiff has suffered significant emotional distress, including anxiety, insomnia, and a decline in her overall mental health, adversely affecting her work performance and quality of life.


IV. CAUSES OF ACTION

11. FIRST CAUSE OF ACTION: Violation of the Anti-Sexual Harassment Act of 1995 (RA 7877). The Defendant’s actions constitute sexual harassment as defined by law, creating a hostile work environment for the Plaintiff.

12. SECOND CAUSE OF ACTION: Violation of the Labor Code. The Defendant's persistent harassment and retaliatory actions undermine the Plaintiff's right to a safe and respectful workplace.

13. THIRD CAUSE OF ACTION: Defamation under the Civil Code. The Defendant’s dissemination of false information about the Plaintiff has harmed her professional reputation and caused emotional harm.


V. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Honorable Court:

  • A. Issue a permanent injunction prohibiting the Defendant from further harassing the Plaintiff;
  • B. Award the Plaintiff monetary damages in the amount of 50,000 pesos for emotional distress and reputational harm;
  • C. Order the Defendant to undergo sensitivity and awareness training regarding workplace harassment;
  • D. Grant the Plaintiff legal costs and attorney’s fees incurred in this action;
  • E. Require a public apology from the Defendant;
  • F. Grant any other relief the Court may deem just and equitable.
